About me

I am a PhD researcher at the University Medical Center Groningen. My project revolves around cognitive decline in the aging population and combines various neuroimaging techniques, including electroencephalography (EEG), magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS). It also involves the use of transcranial alternating current stimulation (tACS) to improve cognition in older adults.

In Indonesia, I work at the Faculty of Pscyhology of Universitas Airlangga as a junior lecturer. In this capacity, I teach Biopsychology of Behavior, Forensic Psychology, and Quantitative Research Methods and Analysis.
